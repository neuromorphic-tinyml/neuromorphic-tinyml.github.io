About

Welcome to the Neuromorphic tinyML Workshop 2022. This two-day workshop (in person) is jointly organized by the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) and Washington University in St. Louis in partnership with McDonnell International Scholars Academy , sponsored by The Scheme for Promotion of Academic and Research Collaboration (SPARC).

  • Workshop On Neuromorphic Systems & tinyML

    Here, we provide the hands-on opportunity to learn and practice state-of-the-art implementations related to tinyML and neuromorphic systems. We invite researchers, faculty members, industry professionals, and students to participate and provide their feedback.

  • Neuromorphic System

    These systems mimic neuro-biological architectures present in the nervous system using physical artificial neurons (made from silicon) to do computations. It is the intersection of Neurophysiology, Computer Science and Electrical Engineering.

  • tinyML

    Big is not always better

    tinyML is a field of study in Machine Learning and Embedded Systems that explores the types of models which can run on small, low-powered devices like microcontrollers. It enables low-latency, and low power model inference at edge devices.

tinyML

Acoustic Classification

Sound detection and classification using machine learning can be used for applications such as keyword spotting, spotting endangered bird spicies, sound signature etc.

tinyRadar

mmWave radar can be used for variety of applications like vital sign monitoring, localization, people counting, object detection and classification.

tinyVision

tinyVision can be used for several applications such as person detection, child minotoring etc., where the data is processed on edge rather than on cloud/PC.

Brain-Computer Interface

These are the devices that enable its users to interact with computers by mean of brain-activity only.
